Economics of Examples: Information and Dynamic Incentives

Example 1 (\(\mathbb{E}[\max\{X_1,X_2\}]\neq\max\{\mathbb{E}(X_1),\mathbb{E}(X_2)\}\)).

Consider support \(x\in[0,1]\), \(X_1\) has density \(f(x)=2x\), and \(X_2\) has density \(g(x)=1\), what's more, we assume \(X_1,X_2\) are independent. The density function of \(\max\{X_1,X_2\}\) can be derived as following: for \(Y=\max\{X_1,X_2\}\), $$ P(Y\leq x)=P(X_1\leq x,X_2\leq x)=P(X_1\leq x)\cdot P(X_2\leq x)=x^3. $$ Hence, the desity function of \(Y\) is \(3x^2\). Therefore, $$ \mathbb{E}[\max\{X_1,X_2\}]=\int_0^1x\cdot 3x^2dx=\frac{3}{4} $$ Meanwhile, $$ \mathbb{E}(X_1)=\int_0^1x\cdot 2xdx=\frac{2}{3},\quad\mathbb{E}(X_2)=\int_0^1 x\cdot 1dx=\frac{1}{2}. $$
